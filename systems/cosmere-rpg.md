# System flavor: Cosmere RPG (Brotherwise Games)

Grounding for the campaign-building AI. Use this to ask questions that fit Brandon Sanderson's Cosmere and how this system runs. The Stormlight (Roshar) Handbook is the released core; the Mistborn (Scadrial) Handbook follows. Campaigns can stay in one world or be "Worldhopper" stories that move between planets.

## Feel and themes
Epic fantasy built on Sanderson's hallmarks: rule-bound magic with real costs, the weight of oaths and promises, sacrifice, leadership, mental health and resilience, and ordinary people becoming legends. Roshar's spirit is captured by ideals like "Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination." Tone runs heroic and emotionally serious without being grim.

## Core mechanic (just enough to ask good questions)
Roll a d20, add a relevant skill, beat a difficulty number. The signature twist is the **Plot Die** — a d6 added on dramatic or risky rolls that produces a **Complication** or an **Opportunity**, throwing narrative and mechanical twists into the scene. Roughly a third of tests should be raised-stakes, so the fiction stays turbulent and surprising. Play happens across three modes: **combat**, **conversation** (social conflict where you wear down an NPC's resolve and they push back), and **endeavors** (extended challenges and downtime projects). A looming-**Events** countdown adds pressure.

## Characters
Instead of classes, characters build along **talent trees** with no multiclass penalty. There are six **heroic paths** — Agent, Envoy, Hunter, Leader, Scholar, Warrior — plus **Radiant paths** (and on Scadrial, **Metalborn** paths) that grant access to Investiture and magical surges. A key early decision is whether the party are mortal heroes, budding magic-users, or on the path to becoming Knights Radiant.

## Magic and its limits (the heart of the Cosmere)
All magic runs on **Investiture**, a finite resource that must be gathered and spent. Each system works differently and each has hard limits: Surgebinding, Allomancy, and Awakening lean on skill tests and spending Investiture; Feruchemy and AonDor give narrower, well-defined effects that reward preparation. Becoming a **Knight Radiant** means bonding a **spren** and speaking the **Ideals** (oaths) of an Order — power is gated behind personal growth and ethical commitment, and breaking faith has consequences. Investiture sources are environmental and limited (Stormlight from highstorms; metals consumed by Allomancers), which keeps the magic grounded.

## Campaign structure the system rewards
Pick a world and an era within it; decide the party's relationship to magic and to any Order or spren bond; build a central conflict worthy of oaths and sacrifice (looming Desolations, political fracture, oppression to overthrow); and seed personal Ideal/growth arcs for each character alongside the external plot. Factions, highprinces or houses, and the cost of power all give the GM levers.

## Good things to ask about as the campaign builds
Which world (Roshar, Scadrial, or a Worldhopper mix) and era; whether PCs are mortal, Metalborn, or Radiant-bound, and which spren/Orders; the central threat and its clock; the political and factional landscape; each character's personal oath or growth arc; how Investiture is sourced and limited in this story; and the tone of raised-stakes moments you want the Plot Die to create.
