# GM Profile — fill this in (it's the heart of the system)

This file is fed into **every** question the builder asks, so its questions are
tailored to *you*, not generic. Spend 15 minutes filling it in honestly — the
more specific you are, the better the questions. Delete the prompts as you go and
replace them with your own answers. (Leaving a section blank is fine; the builder
just leans on what you do give it.)

> Tip: write it the way you'd describe yourself to a new player. First person,
> plain language. There are no wrong answers.

---

## Snapshot
*Who are you as a GM? How long have you run, what systems, how big is your group,
how do you play (in person / online), how much time do you realistically have?*

- 

## How I run
*Your style. Are you an improviser or a planner? Do you anchor campaigns on a
theme or a real-world idea? How do you think about NPCs? Do you give each player
a spotlight moment? Homebrew world or published setting?*

- 

## Growth goals — lean questions here, and teach when useful
*What do you most want to get BETTER at? (e.g., building villains and their
plans, mystery/clue plotting, branching player choices, vivid narration,
spreading prep out, keeping continuity straight.) The builder will steer extra
questions toward these and explain techniques when relevant.*

- 

## Taste
*What do you love? Tone (gritty, heroic, cozy, mythic, comedic). Themes you're
drawn to. The kinds of magic, creatures, factions, and relationships you enjoy.
Inspirations (books, shows, games). What feelings you want your table to walk
away with.*

- 

## Boundaries
*Your lines. Content you never want to appear in questions, tone limits, anything
the builder should steer away from. (Leave permissive or strict — your call.)*

- 

## How to run the daily questions for me
*Practical preferences. Do you like being stretched or eased into things? Daily
bite-sized or weekly chunks? Do you want examples to react to? Which game systems
do you care about first (e.g., D&D 5e, then Cosmere, then Avatar Legends)?*
